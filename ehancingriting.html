Enhancing Writing Skills  & Math Quizzes for Deaf Kids ​

by Aadhil M.M (IT21128240)

BSc (Hons) in Information Technology Specializing in Information Technology​
Sri Lanka Institute of Information Technology

INTRODUCTION


Our project addresses the educational needs of deaf-mute children aged 4 to 8 by developing an interactive and adaptive eLearning tool. The mobile application includes two learning modules: one focused on improving sign language comprehension and the other on enhancing math skills.

In the first module, a sign language GIF is shown, and the child writes the word on paper, takes a photo, and uploads it. The system checks for accuracy and highlights any incorrect letters, supporting both sign language and writing skills.

The second module offers math exercises, including addition, subtraction, and time-telling. Children can answer by typing, using the inbuilt whiteboard, or selecting options, with a progress bar tracking their performance. This app promotes personalized education, improving accessibility and learning outcomes.


RESEARCH PROBLEM


Creating mobile apps for blind children involves addressing key challenges like accessibility, voice recognition accuracy, and user engagement. Effective apps use enhanced audio content, accurate speech recognition for quizzes, and question generation from scanned text to support learning. Parental involvement is crucial for guiding and motivating children. Additionally, apps can help Sinhala-speaking children learn English through interactive, audio-based content, fostering engagement and improving learning outcomes.
