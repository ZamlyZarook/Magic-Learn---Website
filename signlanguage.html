Real-Time Sign Language Translator for Deaf/Mute Kids

by Sameer M.M (IT21120084)

BSc (Hons) in Information Technology Specializing in Information Technology​
Sri Lanka Institute of Information Technology

Intoduction

My part focuses on developing a Real-Time Sign Language Translator for Deaf/Mute children, specifically tailored for Sri Lankan audiences. By integrating American Sign Language (ASL) into the learning process, the application aims to bridge communication gaps and enhance language acquisition through interactive visual aids, including images and videos for both letters and words.



Research Problem

My research problem revolves around the challenge of effectively teaching American Sign Language (ASL) to Sri Lankan deaf children who are primarily familiar with local sign language. The goal is to develop a Real-Time Sign Language Translator that accommodates their learning needs, enhancing communication and language skills through accessible and engaging methods.


Sub Objectives


Sub Objective 1

Dataset Collection: Collect or identify a diverse dataset of ASL hand signs, ensuring it includes various signs, contexts, and variations to enhance model accuracy and robustness.


Sub Objective 2

Deep Learning Model Development: Design and implement a deep learning model architecture suitable for ASL sign recognition, focusing on optimizing performance through techniques such as convolutional neural networks (CNNs).

Sub Objective 3

User-Friendly Interface: Develop a user-friendly interface for the application, allowing users to upload images and receive predictions, ensuring accessibility and ease of use for both children and educators.

Sub Objective 4

Educational Content Integration: Integrate educational content that provides context and usage examples for each ASL sign, enhancing users' understanding and facilitating effective learning.
