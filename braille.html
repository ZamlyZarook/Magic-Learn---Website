Intoduction

Welcome to the official page of my Final Year Research Project at Sri Lanka Institute of Information Technology (SLIIT). In this project my component is aims to make Braille learning mobile application for visually impaired children, with an additional aim to help guardians understand Braille letters.



Research Problem

The primary research problem addressed by this project is the lack of accessible and interactive tools for teaching Braille to visually impaired children, especially in a way that also involves their guardians. Traditional Braille education methods are often limited to printed materials or in-person instruction, which can be challenging for children to engage with independently. Additionally, guardians may struggle to support their child's learning if they are unfamiliar with Braille. This project aims to solve these challenges by providing a mobile application that offers interactive voice-guided lessons and quizzes to enhance the learning experience. The application also integrates image-based Braille recognition, allowing guardians to actively participate by uploading answers, with real-time validation and feedback. This combination of technology and education bridges the gap in accessible Braille learning while promoting parent-child involvement.


Sub Objectives


Sub Objective 1

Create a mobile application with voice-guided lessons that teach Braille letters to visually impaired children in an engaging and accessible way.

Sub Objective 2

Design the app to facilitate the participation of guardians by providing simple explanations of Braille letters and allowing them to assist in quizzes

Sub Objective 3

Utilize image processing techniques to accurately detect Braille patterns from uploaded answers and provide real-time feedback on quiz results.

Sub Objective 4

Host the application on AWS Cloud to guarantee high availability, scalability, and smooth performance for users.
