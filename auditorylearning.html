Audio Lessons and Quizzes for Visually Impaired Kids​

by Wijesundara W.M.R.L (IT21012310)

BSc (Hons) in Information Technology Specializing in Information Technology​
Sri Lanka Institute of Information Technology

Intoduction

Our project addresses the educational needs of visually impaired children aged 4 to 8 by developing an interactive and adaptive eLearning tool. The mobile application offers audio-based lessons and quizzes, creating a multisensory learning experience. By leveraging modern technology, this tool enhances accessibility, engagement, and learning outcomes, providing personalized support for effective education.


Research Problem

Creating mobile apps for blind children involves addressing key challenges like accessibility, voice recognition accuracy, and user engagement. Effective apps use enhanced audio content, accurate speech recognition for quizzes, and question generation from scanned text to support learning. Parental involvement is crucial for guiding and motivating children. Additionally, apps can help Sinhala-speaking children learn English through interactive, audio-based content, fostering engagement and improving learning outcomes.


Sub Objectives


Sub Objective 1

Implement accurate voice recognition to enable children to answer quizzes using voice commands.​

Sub Objective 2

Adapt content to be culturally relevant to Sinhala-speaking children, making lessons more engaging and relatable.​

Sub Objective 3

Develop an intelligent system to scan physical books and generate relevant questions from scanned text.​

Sub Objective 4

Provide Corrective Feedback for Wrong Answers and Encourage Learning Through Error Correction.​
